I have extensive experience and familiarity with the modes and live service design of the NHL franchise. From the original "Hold RT to boost", to the development of a more narrative driven mode, from trying to trade 4 1st Rounders for Brayden Point, to ridiculously detailed arenas compared to my first SNES days, the NHL franchise continues to forge on.

Starting with Donkey Kong Country 2 on the SNES, to 9 years and 5,000 hours on Steam across 106 titles, and 12 years on Xbox playing over 270 titles, gaming continues to grow bigger than ever. Games that push diversity, accessibility, and leverage cutting edge technical solutions in a live game have seen massive growth. Gamers expect their games to live and breath, and the updates to react to their feedback.

At the same time being a dev has gotten harder than ever. Issues are infinitely more complex and require more man hours to fix, and every flaw and bug will surface on social media at some point. In addition third parties are now a major part of gaming, something EA Sports has always had to deal with.
